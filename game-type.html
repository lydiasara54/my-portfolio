Types of Rugby Games

  1. Rugby Union: The most popular form of rugby, played with 15 players on each team. Matches are 80 minutes long and include set pieces like scrums and line-outs.
  2. Rugby League: Played with 13 players on each team. This version features a different scoring system and fewer stoppages, making for a faster-paced game.
  3. Rugby Sevens: A variation of rugby union with 7 players per team and shorter matches, typically 7 minutes per half. It's known for its speed and wide-open style of play.
  4. Rugby Tens: Also known as "Ten-a-side rugby," it features 10 players on each team. It combines elements of both sevens and fifteen-a-side rugby.
  5. Touch Rugby: A minimal-contact version of rugby where players "touch" their opponents instead of tackling. Itâ€™s popular for casual and mixed-gender games.
  6. Tag Rugby: Similar to touch rugby but players wear tags that must be removed to indicate a tackle. It's often played by children and in training sessions.
  7. Wheelchair Rugby: A version of rugby for athletes with disabilities, played on a hard court. It combines elements of rugby, basketball, and handball.
