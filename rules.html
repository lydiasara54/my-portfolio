Basic Rugby Rules

  1. The Objective: The main objective is to score more points than the opposing team by carrying, passing, kicking, and grounding the ball.
  2. Players: Each team consists of 15 players: 8 forwards and 7 backs.
  3. Match Duration: A match is divided into two halves, each lasting 40 minutes, with a 10-minute halftime interval.
  4. Scoring:
    • Try: 5 points awarded for grounding the ball in the opponent's in-goal area.
    • Conversion: 2 points awarded for a successful kick through the goalposts after a try.
    • Penalty Kick: 3 points awarded for a successful penalty kick through the goalposts.
    • Drop Goal: 3 points awarded for a successful drop kick through the goalposts during open play.
  5. The Ball: The ball must be passed backward. Forward passes are not allowed.
  6. Tackling: Players can tackle the ball carrier to stop their progress. Tackles must be made below the shoulders.
  7. Scrum: A method of restarting play involving players packing closely together and pushing against each other to win possession of the ball.
  8. Line-Out: A method of restarting play after the ball has gone out of bounds. Players line up and the ball is thrown back into play.
  9. Ruck: A phase of play where one or more players from each team, who are on their feet and in physical contact, close around the ball on the ground.
  10. Maul: A phase of play where the ball carrier is held by one or more opponents, and one or more of the ball carrier's teammates bind on the ball carrier.
  11. Offside: Players must be behind the ball or the last player on their team who played the ball. Being offside is penalized.
  12. Penalties: Infractions result in penalties, which can include penalty kicks, free kicks, or scrums.
